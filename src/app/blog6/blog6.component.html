When to Remove Wisdom Teeth

Signs that it's time for wisdom tooth extraction and what to expect

Wisdom teeth, also known as third molars, often cause discomfort and dental problems if they don’t have enough space to emerge properly. Recognizing the signs early can prevent pain, infection, and misalignment of other teeth.

1. Persistent Pain or Discomfort

Continuous pain in the back of your mouth, jaw swelling, or tenderness may indicate impacted or misaligned wisdom teeth.

2. Swollen or Bleeding Gums

Inflammation, redness, or bleeding around the wisdom tooth area often signals infection or difficulty in cleaning.

3. Difficulty Opening Mouth or Chewing

If your jaw feels tight or it’s painful to bite and chew, your wisdom teeth could be causing pressure or misalignment.

4. Bad Breath or Unpleasant Taste

Food particles trapped behind partially erupted teeth can cause bacteria growth, leading to bad breath or a foul taste.

5. Regular Dental Checkups

Even if symptoms aren’t severe, X-rays during routine visits can reveal impacted wisdom teeth that may need removal to prevent future problems.
